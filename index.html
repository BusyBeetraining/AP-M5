Essential Tools: Google Calendar & Calendly

At Buzzworks VA, we primarily use two powerful scheduling tools. Understanding how to leverage both will make your life easier and your booking process more professional.

📅
Google Calendar

Best For: Manual booking, internal scheduling, detailed appointment management

Key Features:

  • Create and edit events instantly
  • Set automated reminders
  • Share calendars with team members
  • Color-code appointment types
  • Add detailed notes and attachments
Pro Tip: Always check for conflicts across all shared calendars before booking.
🔗
Calendly

Best For: Self-scheduling, eliminating back-and-forth, automated booking confirmations

Key Features:

  • Prospects book directly from available times
  • Automatically syncs with Google Calendar
  • Built-in reminder emails and SMS
  • Custom booking questions
  • Buffer time between appointments
Pro Tip: Send Calendly links for warm leads who are ready to book but want flexibility in choosing their time.
⚠️ Important: Always Double-Check

Even when using automated tools like Calendly, always verify the agent's calendar before sending booking links. Calendly syncs, but last-minute changes might not reflect immediately.

Critical Rule: Buffer Time Between Appointments

Never book appointments back-to-back. Your agent needs breathing room to prepare, travel, take notes, and handle unexpected situations. Here's the standard buffer time protocol:

📞
Phone Consultations

Buffer: 15 minutes

Quick calls need minimal buffer, but agents still need time to review notes and decompress.

💻
Zoom Meetings

Buffer: 20-30 minutes

Virtual meetings require setup time, tech checks, and follow-up note-taking.

🏠
In-Person Meetings

Buffer: 30-45 minutes

Account for travel time, parking, and running overtime on engaging conversations.

🗝️
Property Showings

Buffer: 45-60 minutes

Travel between properties, lockbox access, and unexpected showing extensions require substantial buffer.

💡 Why Buffer Time Matters: An over-scheduled agent is a stressed agent. They'll rush through meetings, provide lower quality service, and be late to appointments. Buffer time isn't wasted time—it's quality assurance time. Your agent will thank you for protecting their sanity and their reputation.

The 8-Step Booking Process

Follow this exact process every single time you book an appointment. No shortcuts. This system ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

1

Confirm Appointment Time with Prospect

"Based on what we discussed, I'd like to get you on our agent's calendar. Do mornings or afternoons work better for you?"

Offer 2-3 specific options: "Does Tuesday at 2 PM or Thursday at 10 AM work better?"

2

Check Agent's Calendar

Open Google Calendar or Calendly and verify the time slot is actually available. Check for:

  • Existing appointments
  • Buffer time before/after other meetings
  • Agent's blocked-off personal time
  • Travel time if back-to-back in-person meetings
3

Create Calendar Event

In Google Calendar, create a new event with:

  • Title: "[Lead Name] - [Appointment Type]" (e.g., "John Smith - Seller Consultation")
  • Date & Time: Exact start and end time
  • Location: Zoom link, phone number, or property address
4

Add Prospect's Contact Information

In the calendar event description, include:

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Property address (if applicable)
5

Include Notes from Qualification

Add all relevant details from your qualification call:

  • Reason for buying/selling
  • Timeline and urgency level
  • Budget or price range
  • Key preferences or concerns
  • Any special circumstances

Example: "Inherited property, needs to sell within 60 days for estate settlement. Property needs minor repairs. Open to cash offer."

6

Set Reminders

Configure two automated reminders:

  • 24 hours before: Email reminder to prospect and agent
  • 1 hour before: Email/SMS reminder to prospect and agent

Pro tip: Also manually call high-value appointments 24 hours before to confirm.

7

Send Calendar Invite to Prospect

Add the prospect as a guest to the calendar event so they receive:

  • Calendar invite they can add to their own calendar
  • Automatic reminders
  • Easy way to reschedule if needed

Also send a confirmation email (see templates below) with all appointment details.

8

Confirm with Agent

Send a quick message to your agent:

"Hi [Agent Name], I've booked a seller consultation for you on Tuesday, Oct 22 at 2:00 PM with John Smith. He's looking to sell his inherited property within 60 days. Property needs minor repairs. All details are in the calendar event. Let me know if you need anything else!"

Setting Up Automated Reminders

Automated reminders dramatically improve show rates. Here's how to set them up properly in both Google Calendar and Calendly:

Google Calendar Reminders

Step-by-Step Setup:
  1. Open the calendar event you created
  2. Click "Add notification" or "Add reminder"
  3. Set first reminder: "1 day before" via email
  4. Click "+ Add notification" again
  5. Set second reminder: "1 hour before" via email
  6. For high-value appointments, add a third reminder: "1 hour before" via SMS (if available)
  7. Save the event

Calendly Automated Reminders

Setting Default Reminders:
  1. Log into Calendly account
  2. Go to "Event Types" and select the appointment type
  3. Navigate to "Notifications and Cancellation Policy"
  4. Enable "Email Reminders"
  5. Set reminder times: 24 hours and 1 hour before
  6. Customize the reminder message (use templates above)
  7. Enable SMS reminders if available in your plan
  8. Save settings
⚠️ Don't Rely on Automation Alone for Hot Leads

For appointments with highly motivated prospects (HOT leads), make a personal confirmation call 24 hours before. This human touch increases show rates by 20-30% compared to automated reminders alone.

Rescheduling Appointments Professionally

Rescheduling is inevitable. How you handle it determines whether the prospect still shows up eventually or becomes a no-show statistic.

✅
First Reschedule

Response: Accommodating

"No problem at all! I completely understand. Let me find you a new time. What day works better for you?"

Action: Offer 2-3 alternative times immediately

⚠️
Second Reschedule

Response: Understanding but Firm

"I understand things come up. I want to make sure we book a time you can definitely keep. When are you 100% certain you'll be available?"

Action: Get a stronger commitment; verify nothing will interfere

🚩
Third Reschedule

Response: Respectfully Direct

"I sense this might not be the right time for you. That's totally fine. Would you prefer I follow up with you in [timeframe] when things settle down?"

Action: Put them in nurture campaign; don't chase endlessly

Rescheduling Best Practices

Respond quickly - Acknowledge reschedule requests within 1 hour
Be empathetic - Don't make them feel bad; life happens
Offer options immediately - Have 2-3 new times ready to suggest
Update all systems - Change calendar, CRM, and notify agent
Send new confirmation - Treat it like a fresh booking with new reminders
Note the pattern - Track chronic reschedulers in CRM

Handling Cancellations

Cancellations sting, but how you handle them can keep the door open for future opportunities.

The Cancellation Call Script

When a prospect calls to cancel:

Step 1 - Acknowledge:

"I understand. Thanks for letting me know."

Step 2 - Probe Gently:

"Can I ask what changed? I want to make sure there's nothing we could have done differently."

Step 3 - Offer Alternative:

If they're not ready now: "Would it make sense to reconnect in [timeframe]?"

If they found another agent: "I understand. If things don't work out, we're here to help."

If they're unsure: "Would a quick 15-minute phone call help you decide? Less commitment than a full appointment."

Step 4 - End Professionally:

"I appreciate your time, [Name]. We're here if you need us in the future. Best of luck with everything!"

Types of Cancellations & How to Handle Them

📅
Life Happened

"Emergency came up," "Got sick," "Family matter"

Response: Immediately offer to reschedule. These are still viable leads.

🏢
Found Another Agent

"I'm going with someone else," "Family friend is an agent"

Response: Gracefully exit. Add to long-term nurture list in case it doesn't work out.

❄️
Cold Feet

"Not ready yet," "Need more time to think," "Not sure anymore"

Response: They were never truly qualified. Add to cold lead nurture campaign.

👻
No-Show (Silent Cancel)

Didn't show up, no call, no explanation

Response: One follow-up call: "Hi, I noticed you missed our appointment. Everything okay?" If no response, move to cold list.

⚠️ When to Let Go

Don't chase leads who:

  • Cancel 3+ times without rescheduling
  • No-show twice without explanation
  • Tell you they're working with another agent
  • Ask you to stop contacting them

Your time is valuable. Focus on leads who want to be helped.
